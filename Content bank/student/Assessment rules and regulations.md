# 1.INTRODUCTION

These Rules and Regulations are established to provide clear guidance on the conduct of academic assessments at Eddings University (EUNI). The University adopts a Grade Point Average (GPA) Assessment System as the standard method for evaluating student performance across all academic programs.

The purpose of this document is to:

1.  Ensure fairness, transparency, and consistency in the assessment of students.
2.  Define the principles and procedures governing academic evaluation.
3.  Uphold academic integrity and excellence in alignment with EUNI’s mission and vision.
4.  These regulations apply to all students enrolled in undergraduate, postgraduate, diploma, and certificate programs offered by EUNI, effective from the date of publication.

## 1.1 Objectives of the GPA Assessment System

The GPA Assessment System is designed to:

1.  Measure student performance objectively and quantitatively.
2.  Provide a standardized framework for grading across all programs and faculties.
3.  Promote continuous learning and accountability among students.
4.  Enable students to track academic progress and identify areas for improvement.
5.  Maintain the credibility of EUNI’s academic awards in line with national and international higher education standards.

## 1.2 Scope of Application

These rules and regulations cover:

1.  Calculation and interpretation of GPA.
2.  End of programme assessment
3.  Supplementary examination, carryover, and repeat
4.  Withdrawal
5.  Provisions for re-assessment and appeals.

# 2\. CREDIT LOAD

For any one semester, a student shall take a minimum of 15 credit hours. The load of credits may vary for the different Diplomas, Degrees, and Certificates in the different years of study as do other special requirements such as choice of courses, standard of achievement, compulsory courses, pre-requisites, attachments and teaching practice. Students are advised, therefore, to consult the respective programmes. At the end of the programme of study, a student is expected to have accumulated a minimum of 120 credit hours.

Normally, a semester course is worth 3 credit hours. In some cases, different courses, may be worth less or more than 3 credit hours. However, a student is required to register for the prescribed Faculty courses a total minimum of 15 credit hours per semester.

# 3\. ASSESSMENT OF PERFORMANCE

Assessment for the Four-Year Degree Programmes at GUI is based on Letter Grades and the Grade Point Average (GPA) system. Assessment of students' performance is based on all courses registered for in a semester, at the end of the year, and upon completion of the programme of study. Assessment may be based on tests, essays, practicals, assignments, etc.

## **3.1 Assessment of practical, attachment and degree projects**.

No student shall be allowed to graduate without fulfilling requirements for practicals, attachment and degree project as required by the relevant programmes.

## 3.2 Grading System

The grading system for student performance shall consist of six basic grades, A, B, C, D E, and F as indicated in Table 1. Other descriptions shall be:

I (incomplete/deferred)

FW (withdrawal).

## 3.3 Withdrawal from a programme of study

Students may withdraw temporarily or permanently from a programme of study by a written application and approval. A student who discontinues attendance in a programme of study without official withdrawal receives grades of “F” in the courses. In all cases, a student shall be allowed to withdraw for a maximum period of one academic year and only once in their programme of study. The following are acceptable reasons for a student to withdraw:

1.  Withdrawal on Financial grounds, compassionate grounds (FW)
2.  Withdrawal on Health grounds (HW) (Special consideration may be given in cases of serious illnesses, which has been certified by a University doctor, and the student may be allowed to withdrawal for a maximum of two academic years).
3.  Pregnancy Withdrawal (PW)
4.  Voluntary withdrawal (VW)

A student must submit a written application and obtain a written approval before withdrawing from a programme. A student cannot withdraw for more than one academic year without seeking further written approval.

## 3.4 Failing a course

A student shall be deemed to have failed a course if he/she obtains a grade below C- in that course. A student who absents him/herself from an assessment session (test, examination, practicals, etc) in any course without a valid reason shall get a grade of F.

**3.5. Complete course grade**

When the quality of course work is satisfactory but some minor requirement of the course has not been completed for reasons acceptable to the instructor and the university, a report of “I” may be made and additional time granted for completion of work. To remove an Incomplete “I”, a student must complete the deficiency within the allotted time and the instructor will submit the appropriate grade to the College Academic Assessment Committee. An Incomplete “I” not removed before the beginning of classes in the subsequent year becomes an “F”.

## 3.6 Deferred Examinations

**3.6.1** Students who fail deferred examinations will be treated in the same manner as those who failed end of Semester examination and shall be eligible for referred examination if they qualify.

**3.6.2** Student's illness must be supported by medical evidence. This evidence should normally be given to the university prior to the examination.

## 3.7 Missing Examinations

**3.7.1** Normally the only valid reasons for missing examinations are illness and bereavement. In particular, extramural activities and misreading of the examination time table are not valid reasons.

**3.7.2** Students who miss an examination without a valid reason will be awarded a grade of 'F' for that examination.

**3.8 Appeals**

**3.8.1** Any appeal against withdrawal on academic grounds should within three weeks after the publication of results be directed to the Vice-Chancellor who will in turn direct it to the Appeals Committee.

**3.8.2** There shall be a standing Appeals Committee which shall hear and decide on appeals against the decision of Senate to withdraw students.

**3.8.3** Students who wish to request that a particular grade be investigated or examination be remarked should direct their request to the Vice Chancellor who shall refer the matter to the Appeals Committee. Supplementary examinations shall not be remarked as these constitute a re-assessment.

**3.8.4** The Head of Department of the Lecturer affected by the appeal or his/her nominee shall be invited as a witness.

**3.8.5** The appellant shall be heard on his/her appeal either orally or in writing and he/she shall bear the cost of appearing for the purpose of being heard.

## 4.2 End of Year Assessment

### 4.2.1 Pass and Proceed Cases

A student who has an end of academic year cumulative grade point average (GPA) of at least 2.00 and no grade below C- in the courses prescribed and registered for the level is deemed to have passed at that level and will proceed to the next level.

### 4.2.2 Referrals/Supplementary Examinations

1.  Students with GPA of 1.50 or more and have grades below C- in some course(s) will be referred in the failed course(s) as long as the student is not repeating the level of the failed courses.
2.  A student shall proceed to the next level if he/she passes all the referred courses. The maximum pass grade achieved after a supplementary examination shall be the equivalent of Grade C-.

**4.2.3 Carryovers**

Students who fail referred course(s) and whose new recalculated GPA is equal to or greater than 2.00 shall be allowed to carryover (CO) the failed course(s) to the next level as long as the total failed load does not exceed 4 credit hours.

### 4.2.4 Repeats

1.  Throughout this document the word "repeat" (RPT) shall refer to repeating on academic grounds only.
2.  Students who fail referred course(s) and their recalculated GPA is equal or greater than 1.50 shall repeat the level in the course(s) failed provided that the student does not qualify for a carryover and has not repeated before.
3.  Students who fail CO course(s) shall qualify for a repeat at the lower level without option of a referral in the failed CO provided that they do not simultaneously qualify for a repeat or withdrawal in the main level of study.

### 4.2.5 Conditions Imposed on Students Who Have Carryovers

1.  Students shall carryover the same course once during a particular programme of study.
2.  Examinations of carryover courses shall be taken at the next immediate sitting of such examination(s). The onus of checking the scheduling and registering for such examination’s rests with the student. The maximum pass grade achieved after a carryover examination shall be equivalent to Grade C-.
3.  A student with carryover courses may attend the failed courses if the timetable permits him/her to do so. The onus of preparing for the examination rests with the student.
4.  The progression of a student to the next level shall depend on the outcomes of both CO and the main level of study.
5.  Students with CO will not be permitted to change or substitute courses to which the carryover refers.
6.  Students who fail a carryover and qualify for another carryover in the main level of study shall repeat the failed carryover course in the lower level and simultaneously register for the carryover course in the main level. After passing both courses, s/he shall proceed to the next level of study.
7.  There shall be no option for referral after failing a CO.

### 4.2.6 Conditions Imposed on Students Who are Repeating the Year

1.  Posted examination results during the repeat year shall be the actual grades obtained in each course examined during that year but will carry a letter “RPT” in brackets to the right of the grade to indicate the grade from repeated course(s).
2.  Students shall repeat only once during their studies in the University. Students who fail courses they are repeating shall be withdrawn.
3.  Candidates repeating a level, may, at the discretion of concerned Head of Department with the approval of the Dean of the relevant
4.  Faculty (in which the subject is offered), be allowed to register for a
5.  different course other than the failed course during the repeat level. The registration form shall carry a written approval as an attachment. Those who have been allowed to switch to another course shall still be deemed to repeat the level of study and the description "RPT" shall apply.

## 4.2.7Withdrawal

1.  Students whose GPA at the end of the level is less than 1.50 shall be withdrawn.
2.  Students who fail a repeated course (regardless of the credit load of the failed course(s)) during the repeating level shall be withdrawn without an option of a referral.
3.  Students who fail carryover examinations and simultaneously satisfy conditions of repeat in the main level of study shall be withdrawn.
4.  Students who are repeating a course(s) and simultaneously have a carryover course shall be withdrawn should they fail either of the courses.

# 5 END OF PROGRAMME ASSESSMENT

End of Programme Assessment is done in the final level of the 4-Year Bachelor’s degree programme using the Letter Grades and the GPA. The quality of the award is based on the cumulative GPA from years 2 to 4 of the programme of study. The GPA to determine the quality of the award for students who join the college at third year level, shall be based on the cumulative GPA for year 3 and year 4. For other programmes of duration less than four years, such as one year honours degree programmes, the quality of the degrees shall be based on the actual duration of the programme of study.

# 6\. CALCULATION OF GPAs

## 6.1 Semester GPA (sGPA)

Assessment of students' performance for a given semester will be based on letter grades interpreted from corresponding raw marks and grade points scored from each course as outlined below.

1.  Grade points are ordered performance scale points a student can obtain in a course.
2.  A semester grade point average (GPA) is the weighted average of grade points earned in the semester. The semester GPA is calculated by dividing the sum of grade points earned multiplied by the corresponding credit hours by the total number of credit hours attempted whether the course was passed or not, exclusive of credits reported incomplete (I); and withdrawn (W).

For a given semester GPA is computed to assess the students’ performance using the following formula
