**EXECUTIVE SUMMARY**

Eddings University (EU) have conducted a Self-Assessment in preparation of NCHE accreditation. .

The University operates as a dual-mode institution, offering both face-to-face and ODeL programmes. The assessment was guided by the NCHE Minimum Standards and accreditation assessment tools for both face to face and ODeL. The tools guided the assessment team in assessing the governance and management, financial resources and sustainability of the institution, availability and functionality of the infrastructure (buildings, space and other physical facilities), the provision of the library and other learning resources, the provision of water and sanitation facilities, provision of student support services, the relevance and quality of the design of the programmes, the adequacy of the academic staff complement for delivering the programme, compliance with the admission criteria for the programme and quality assurance systems.

Findings of the assessment revealed strengths. EU has guiding principles, namely, the vision, mission, core values and strategic objectives in place to provide effective direction for its operations. There are effective tools for monitoring and evaluating the intended progress towards achieving the vision, mission, and goals of the institution. In addition, the University has functional governance structures such as Council, Senate, and Management to guide the implementation of the vision, mission and strategic objectives of the University. The University has key policies and regulations to provide implementation framework. In addition, the University operates according to its Financial Regulations which provide effective guidelines for its financial activities. The current annual budget available showed allocations to the institution’s strategic activities of learning and research among other things. More importantly, the University keeps up-to-date with its financial audit requirements. There was also evidence that academic staff are engaged in practical and scholarly research and publications. Evidence of collaborative research with other local and international institutions was available.

Areas requiring improvement were also identified. For instance, the ODeL component though significant, is not clearly reflected in the mission of the institution. The organogram does not clearly specify ODeL’s management responsibility and the management of funds collected through ODeL students. Persistent cases of missing grades for students was also one area that requires urgent improvement. Inadequate classroom and library spaces leading to overcrowding was also another area that requires improvement. There is need for routine maintenance of all physical facilities as well as sanitation and hygiene in all areas used by students. For example, cafeteria, washrooms, laundry, and commercial business areas. Monitoring quality of students’ accommodation and 24/7 water supply is required. The University also need to consider constructing indoor and outdoor sports facilities including wellness centre matching the growing number of students and staff. There is also need to fill up gaps in library staffing. The curriculum document (full programme document) is out of reach of students although students upon being registered for the programme are provided course/modules outlines with assessment procedures. It was also noted that Information resources for the Bachelor of Environmental Management are insufficient and some are outdated. Minimal use of LMS functionalities like discussion forums and video conferencing was also observed.

- **1.0 INTRODUCTION**

In line with the National Council for Higher Education (NCHE) requirements for accreditation assessment, Eddings University conducted a self-assessment in preparation the accreditation assessment.

The assessment was guided by the NCHE minimum standards and accreditation assessment tools for both face-to-face and ODeL. The tools guided the reviewers in assessing of governance and management, financial resources and sustainability of the institution, availability and functionality of the infrastructure (buildings, space and other physical facilities), the provision of the library and other learning resources, the provision of water and sanitation facilities, provision of student support services, the relevance and quality of the design programmes, the adequacy of the academic staff complement for delivering the programme, compliance with the admission criteria for the programme and quality assurance systems.

- **1.1Approach**

The assessment involved the following methods: observations through inspection of facilities and premises, document review, and interviews. The review team visited and inspected various facilities and premises, including classrooms, the library, laboratories, offices, students’ hostels, and sanitation facilities. For the document review, the team collected data by examining policy documents, meeting minutes, programme curriculum documents, staff personal files, and other relevant documents. Interviews with students, staff, and management were also conducted. The data were analysed to determine if the institution and academic programme assessed met the set minimum standards for higher education required for accreditation by NCHE.

- **2.0 INSTITUTIONAL BACKGROUND, GOVERNANCE STRUCTURES AND MANAGEMENT**
- **2.1 Institutional Background**

Eddings University is a privately-owned higher education institution established in 2010 by Malawian proprietors. The University was founded with the objective of providing quality higher education through both conventional face-to-face teaching and Open and Distance e-Learning (ODeL) modes of delivery, thereby expanding access to tertiary education for diverse categories of learners.

The institution was last accredited by the National Council for Higher Education (NCHE) in 2017, together with its approved academic programmes. Since that accreditation, the University has continued to operate under the regulatory framework governing higher education institutions in Malawi, offering programmes in accordance with the conditions and standards set by the NCHE.

As a locally owned private university, Eddings University aims to contribute to national human capital development by providing accessible, relevant, and quality higher education programmes that respond to the educational and workforce needs of Malawi.
