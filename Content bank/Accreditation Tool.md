## **INTRODUCTION** 

The National Council for Higher Education (NCHE) is mandated to register and accredit all higher education institutions and academic programmes in Malawi. One of the steps of the accreditation process is the assessment of the institution offering programmes. 

Institutional assessment is informed by guidelines and standards for accreditation of higher education institutions and minimum requirements set by the Council. This instrument is for assessing institution’s compliance with minimum standards. 

## **AIM OF ASSESSMENT** 

The aim of assessment is to assist reviewers make well-informed judgment on the capacity of an institution to offer academic programmes. 

## **Specific objectives** 

The following are specific objectives of the assessment: 

- a) Assess governance and management of an institution; 

- b) Assess the financial resources and sustainability of the institution; 

- c) Assess the availability and functionality of the infrastructure (buildings, space and other physical facilities); 

- d) Assess the provision of the library and other learning resources; 

- e) Assess the provision of water and sanitation facilities; 

- f) Assess provision of student support services; 

- g) Assess the suitability and functionality of other physical infrastructures (including the availability and suitability of classroom and laboratory space); and 

- h) Assess quality assurance systems. 

## **DATA COLLECTION METHODS FOR THE ASSESSMENT EXERCISE** 

- a) Reviewing documents and other relevant documentary evidence such as policy documents and publications 

- b) Inspecting facilities and premises. 

- c) Interviewing staff and students. 

## **DATA ANALYSIS AND PRESENTATION** 

Appropriate quantitative and qualitative data analysis methods shall be used. All tables shall be accompanied by suitable qualitative descriptions.

## **ACCREDITATION DECISIONS** 

- a) **Accreditation of an institution** : Granted for an institution that has met minimum requirements. The decision may sometimes include recommendations for eliminating residual shortcomings. Where a decision includes some remedial conditions, the decision may be referred to as accreditation with conditions.  Accreditation with conditions shall specify the requirements and the time period (one year) within which the shortcomings could be addressed. 

- b) **Denial of Accreditation** : Where an institution has not satisfied (does not comply with) requirements on initial registration or upon institution assessment carried out for the purpose of accreditation. Denial of accreditation shall indicate that the institution has serious shortcomings. 

- c) **Withdrawal of Accreditation:** Where a previously accredited institution fails to maintain requirements upon another round of assessments for accreditation.
